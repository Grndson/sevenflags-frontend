Travel Package

Embark on an unforgettable 14-day journey to Kenya with Seven Flags Investments and Property Management, expertly blending immersive business opportunities with the wonders of East Africa. This exclusive travel package is thoughtfully designed for Black Americans seeking to enrich their knowledge and experience of Kenya's vibrant culture, breathtaking wildlife, and stunning coastlines from a global perspective; while exploring the Real Estate Investment opportunities of East Africa.

Brief Itinerary Summary

  • Day 0: International Flight – NY, USA to Nairobi, Kenya
  • Day 1–3: Nairobi – City Tours, Elephant Sanctuary, Nairobi National Park, Giraffe Centre
  • Day 4–5: Kisumu – City Tour, Boat Ride on Lake Victoria
  • Day 6: Nakuru – Game Drive at Lake Nakuru National Park
  • Day 7–8: Naivasha – Boat Safari, Crescent Island, Hell's Gate
  • Day 9–10: Masai Mara – Full Safari Experience, Optional Balloon Ride & Maasai Village Visit
  • Day 11–12: Ukunda/Diani – Coastal Beach Stay, Fort Jesus & Mombasa Old Town Tour
  • Day 13: Return to Nairobi – Business Presentation & Farewell Dinner
  • Day 14: Departure – Airport Transfer

Detailed Day-by-Day Itinerary

0

International Departure

Set off from the USA on a comfortable, overnight international flight to Kenya, your gateway to adventure and opportunity.

1-3

Nairobi – Gateway to Africa

Arrive in Nairobi, Kenya's bustling capital. Settle into your hotel before exploring local markets, museums, and the city's thriving business district. Enjoy guided tours of the David Sheldrick Elephant Sanctuary, home to rescued orphan elephants, and the Giraffe Centre, where you can interact with endangered giraffes. Experience a classic African safari in Nairobi National Park, spotting lions, rhinos, and more, all within view of the city skyline.

4-5

Kisumu – Heart of the Lake Region

Travel to Kisumu, a vibrant city on the shores of Lake Victoria. Immerse yourself in local life during a city tour, then take a serene boat ride across Africa's largest lake, witnessing remarkable birdlife and fishermen at work. Discover Kisumu's role as a regional commercial hub and connect with local entrepreneurs.

6

Nakuru – Flamingos and Wildlife

Journey to Nakuru for a game drive in Lake Nakuru National Park, famed for its flocks of flamingos and diverse wildlife including rhinos, leopards, and buffalo. Marvel at the park's picturesque landscapes and learn about conservation efforts.

7-8

Naivasha – Nature and Adventure

Next, venture to Naivasha for a boat safari on Lake Naivasha, observing hippos, fish eagles, and more. Explore Crescent Island on foot, walking among giraffes, zebras, and antelopes in an open sanctuary. Experience the dramatic scenery of Hell's Gate National Park, where you can hike, bike, and marvel at geothermal features.

9-10

Masai Mara – The Ultimate Safari

Delve into the Masai Mara, Kenya's premier wildlife reserve. Enjoy thrilling game drives with opportunities to see the Big Five: lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhino. Choose an optional hot air balloon ride for breathtaking sunrise views over the savannah, then visit a Maasai village to learn about traditional culture and crafts.

11-12

Ukunda/Diani – Sun, Sea & History

Fly to Kenya's stunning southern coast to unwind on the white sands of Diani Beach. Relax in luxurious seaside accommodation and swim in the warm Indian Ocean. Take a guided excursion to historic Fort Jesus and the atmospheric streets of Mombasa Old Town, rich in Swahili-Arabic heritage.

13

Return to Nairobi – Business & Farewell

Conclude your journey with a return to Nairobi, where you'll participate in a comprehensive business presentation by Seven Flags Investments and Property Management. Review insights gained during your travels over a festive farewell dinner, celebrating new partnerships and friendships.

14

Departure

Enjoy a leisurely breakfast before your private airport transfer. Depart from Nairobi, carrying with you memories of adventure, discovery, meaningful connections, and hopefully an initial investment in Kenya.
