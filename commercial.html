Commercial Real Estate

Nairobi's Commercial Real Estate Market

Nairobi stands as East Africa's economic powerhouse, experiencing unprecedented growth in commercial real estate. The city's strategic position as a regional hub, combined with robust economic fundamentals, has created exceptional investment opportunities across multiple property sectors.

Office Space

Prime commercial districts drive demand for Class A office spaces.

  • Central Business District (CBD) - Premium Grade A offices
  • Westlands - Modern corporate headquarters
  • Upper Hill - Financial district expansion
  • Kilimani - Emerging business hub

Retail Sector

Expanding middle class fuels demand for modern retail spaces and shopping centers.

  • Two Rivers Mall - Luxury retail destination
  • Sarit Centre - Established shopping hub
  • The Hub - Karen's premium retail center
  • Neighborhood centers - Growing suburban demand

Industrial & Logistics

Strategic location and infrastructure development create opportunities in warehousing and logistics.

  • Mombasa Road - Major logistics corridor
  • Athi River - Industrial park development
  • Jomo Kenyatta International Airport vicinity
  • Standard Gauge Railway integration

Hospitality & Mixed-Use

Tourism growth and business travel drive demand for hospitality and mixed-use developments.

  • Business hotels in commercial districts
  • Serviced apartments for expatriates
  • Mixed-use developments
  • Conference and event facilities

Economic & Demographic Drivers

Kenya's robust economic growth, expanding population, and strategic regional position create a compelling foundation for commercial real estate investment. Infrastructure development and regional integration further enhance the investment landscape.
